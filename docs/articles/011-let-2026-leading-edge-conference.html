準備迎接 LET 2026
Get set for LET 2026

This event article previews the 20th IWA Leading Edge Conference on Water and Wastewater Technologies in Houston, focusing on resilient water technologies.

中文完整摘要說明

本文預告 2026 年 6 月 1-5 日將於美國 Houston 舉行的第 20 屆 IWA Leading Edge Conference on Water and Wastewater Technologies。會議主題為「變動世界中的韌性水」，聚焦 PFAS 礦化、數位化與 AI、膜技術、海水淡化與濃鹽水管理、直接飲用水再利用、模組化與分散式污水處理，以及能源中性的固體與污水管理。文章也介紹 Rice University 作為創新場域，強調分子與奈米尺度水技術、電氣化、自動化與去中心化處理對未來安全且可負擔水與衛生服務的重要性。

中文全文

本文介紹第 20 屆 IWA Leading Edge Conference on Water and Wastewater Technologies，會議將於 2026 年 6 月 1-5 日在美國 Texas, Houston 舉行。2026 年 LET 以「Resilient Water in a Changing World」為主題，延續 2024 年德國 Essen 第 19 屆會議的成果。LET 的特色在於聚焦最前沿的科學、技術創新與領先實務，討論它們如何塑造水管理的重大轉型。 2026 年議程主題包括飲用水、污水、污泥與濃鹽水處理中的 PFAS 礦化技術；數位化、自動化與人工智慧；膜技術與多功能混合系統；海水淡化、能源與濃鹽水管理；改良型直接飲用水再利用；模組化與分散式污水處理；以及先進固體與污水管理，以推動能源中性的處理廠。 會議將在 Rice University 舉行，該校擁有 Rice WaTER Institute，並鄰近 NASA Johnson Space Center。文章指出，Rice WaTER Institute 已成立 Rice PFAS Abatement and Replacement Center，用於破壞不同基質中的持久性化學物質，也成立 Rice Center for Membrane Excellence，推動膜技術與濃鹽水管理研究。 文章最後強調，技術在水管理發展中扮演關鍵角色。LET 2026 旨在提供接觸前沿科技洞見的平台，激發能創造永續且具韌性未來的轉型解方。

Alvarez tells 7he Source what delegates can expect from the 20th IWA Leading Edge Conference on Water and Wastewater Technologies. he 20th IWA Leading Edge Conference on Water and Wastewater Technologies (LET) will take place in Houston, Texas, USA, on 1-5 June 2026. Focusing on the theme of ‘Resilient Water in a Changing World’, this 2026 LET builds on the success of the 19th edition held in Essen, Germany, in 2024, which gathered around 400 participants from more than 40 countries to network and learn from almost 70 worldclass presentations. What sets LET apart is the way in which the event has been designed and established to be the key conference for sharing the latest insights into how pioneering science, technological innovation and leading practices will shape the major transformations that are taking place in water management. Dynamic programme Key themes of LET 2026 include per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) mineralisation technologies for drinking water, wastewater, sludge and brine treatment; digitalisation, automation and artificial intelligence (Al); membrane technologies and multifunctional hybrid systems; desalination, energy and brine management; improved direct potable reuse; modular and decentralised wastewater treatment; and advanced solids and wastewater management for energy-neutral treatment plants.
