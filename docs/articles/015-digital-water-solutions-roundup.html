中文完整摘要說明

本頁聚焦水務數位化：Severn Trent 以衛星雷達技術監測極端天氣和廢水系統風險；Aquapreneur Innovation Initiative 表揚多家早期水科技公司；Spire Global 與 GIST Research 將建立衣索比亞牧民移動追蹤工具，協助降低氣候壓力與衝突風險；In-Situ 則推出 TurbiTech UVT 感測器，用於優化 UV 消毒與水質監測。

中文全文

Severn Trent 正使用衛星技術追蹤極端天氣，並協助維持廢水系統順暢運作。透過先進合成孔徑雷達衛星，即使在雲層覆蓋下，公用事業也能觀察地面狀況，掌握暴雨與洪水風險，並在問題到達用戶端前提前介入。公司認為，結合衛星資料、既有感測器與分析工具，可提升排水與廢水網路在未來面對洪水與風暴中斷時的透明度與韌性。 Aquapreneur Innovation Initiative 表揚 10 家 Tacking Water Pollution Challenge 得獎者，這是 HCL Group、UpLink 與世界經濟論壇合作推動的早期創新倡議。得獎者包含 EarthFokus、Hohonu、iFLUX、SWAN Systems 與 Xatoms 等，涵蓋即時水資源管理、洪水和水位監測、地下水與污染物流動監測、農業灌溉最佳化，以及以 AI 和量子化學設計光觸媒進行水淨化等方向。 Spire Global 將與 GIST Research 和 IOM 合作，建立衣索比亞索馬利地區的氣候驅動牧民移動研究與追蹤工具。此工具會整合土壤水分洞察與早期預警系統，監測環境和生計壓力，協助政府與夥伴更早介入，降低資源競爭與衝突風險。 In-Situ 推出 TurbiTech UVT 感測器，這是一款自清潔儀器，用於最佳化 UV 消毒控制並監測廢水處理設施中的總懸浮固體。公司表示，感測器可提供即時 UV 透射率量測，協助操作人員降低能源使用與營運成本，並可與資料記錄、顯示與通訊系統整合。

Original Full Text

UK water company Severn Trent is using satellite technology to track extreme weather and keep wastewater systems running smoothly. According to Severn Trent, its work with Earth observation specialists ICEYE will revolutionise how flooding and wastewater issues are managed across the Midlands. Using advanced synthetic aperture radar (SAR) satellites, the utility says it can now see what’s happening on the ground, even through thick cloud cover, providing a bird’s-eye view of flood risks and helping to prevent problems before they reach properties. Richard Walwyn, head of asset intelligence and innovation at Severn Trent, said: “This is a transformational step forward for us. ICEYE’s satellite technology gives us a clear view of what’s happened on the ground during extreme weather despite cloud coverage. Having a better understanding of how inundation affects our assets enables us to plan interventions and optimise how our. network performs to minimise impacts for our customers and the environment.” Severn Trent says it is the first UK water utility company to adopt ICEYE’s technology, and believes this marks a significant shift in how the industry will tackle flooding and storm-related disruptions in the future.
