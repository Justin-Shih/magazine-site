Original Full Text

IWA News Rwanda Chapter recognised as Chapter of the Year The IWA Young Water Professionals (YWP) Rwanda Chapter was recognised as the 2025 YWP Chapter of the Year during the 7th |WA Emerging Water Leaders Forum in Bangkok, Thailand. Throughout 2024, the Rwanda YWP Chapter demonstrated exceptional engagement with the broader WA network. Two members of its steering committee actively participated in the IWA World Water Congress & Exhibition held in Toronto, Canada, strengthening connections between the Rwandan YWP community and global water professionals. The Chapter also collaborated with the IWA Zambia Chapter to deliver a webinar on African youth engagement in advancing SDG6, reflecting its commitment to regional cooperation and shared learning. Furthermore, the Chapter’s coordinator was selected to join the IWA LeaP Leadership Programme, marking a significant milestone in nurturing future leaders within the sector. Innovation Next Generation Sanitation Systems Project teams As part of its efforts to advance practical sanitation options, especially for low- and middle-income countries, IWA has progressed its Next Generation Sanitation Systems (NGSS) project into its next phase, announcing the organisations selected to take forward the project’s research and development work. Train 1 consists of teams from NOAH, Hydrohm, and Recovered Potential. This train focuses on advancing a marketready integrated sanitation system, with an emphasis on deployable, household-scale systems. Train 2 is led by Daqing Anxin Hengye Instrument & Control Equipment and focuses on the development of ceramic membrane filtration, electrocoagulation and electrodisinfection modules. Exceptional professionals receive Water and Development Awards Three pioneering water sector professionals have received the 2025 IWA Water and Development Award, with their awards being presented by IWA President Hamanth Kasan on 8 December during the Opening Ceremony of the Water and Development Congress & Exhibition held in Bangkok, Thailand. Evan Thomas received the 2025 I|WA Water and Development Award for Research for pioneering the integration of research, technology and enterprise to deliver sustainable water security for millions. Combining scientific innovation with social impact, Thomas has developed models linking water delivery in low- and middle-income countries to carbon finance, generating more than 45 million carbon credits and expanding access to safe water for at least five million people; his real-time water quality monitoring technologies, including the first fully integrated internetconnected faecal risk sensor, have also set new standards for transparency. There were two recipients in the Practice category. Professor Meera Mehta was honoured for her pioneering leadership in transforming water and sanitation governance and finance in lowand middle-income countries. Jay Bhagwan was recognised for his leadership in advancing non-sewered sanitation (NSS) innovation through the Water Research Commission’s South African Sanitation Technology Enterprise Programme (SASTEP). IWA President Hamanth Kasan and the three 2025 IWA Water and Development Award winners. From left: Evan Thomas, Meera Mehta, Hamanth Kasan and Jay Bhagwan. On the road to 2026 United Nations Water Conference The high-level preparatory meeting for the 2026 UN Water Conference was held in Dakar, Senegal, in late January, with IWA participating in this key step in advance of December’s UN Water Conference. The team took to Dakar the recently finalised Bangkok Communiqué on Water Security and Resilience - a key output from the |WA Water and Development Congress held at the end of last year - and brought |WA’s 2026 World Water Congress & Exhibition to be included as part of the road map to the UN Water Conference. At the heart of the agenda for the two-day Dakar meeting were sessions to shape the six interactive dialogues that will take place in December.
